NOW AND THEN

The school of Computer Music of the Conservatory “A. Pedrollo” in Vicenza has existed since 2003/04. It was born in the classrooms of the so-called GRAIM (Musical Acoustics and Informatics Research Group), an Electronic Music laboratory founded at the Conservatory a few years earlier with the aim of carrying out research and production. With the opening of an Electronic Music chair in 2003/04, lessons began held by Lorenzo Pagliei, founding teacher of the course.
At the beginning, GRAIM consisted of a single class attended by only four students. After 20 years of activity it is a university course, organized as an independent department, with 4 chairs, 3-4 external contract professors and attended by 48 students. The roll of honor includes 6 diplomas from the old system, 40 first level degrees, 3 second level degrees and 7 thesis holders. The current number of students enrolled is greater than the sum of all the students of the other Electronic Music courses in the region.

COURSE DESCRIPTION

The Electronic Music course covers all aspects of becoming professionals in the vast field of electronic music, and teaches students to operate comprehensively in a wide range of professional situations:

  • Studio productions, from recording of ensembles and musical groups to mixing and mastering;
  • Amplification of complex live events, from musical groups of any kind to contemporary opera theater with specific use of spatialization;
  • Composition of audio, audio/video, interactive and multi-modal electronic works, from the initial conception to the final realization in a concert including writing of the score and documentation;
  • Electroacoustic, interactive and multimodal music performances, from logistical organization to final realization;
  • Technical assistance to artists, composers, companies or cultural operators to carry out their project using technologies, from design to the creation of executive environments for installations, interactive stands and dance or music performances;
  • Sound production and creation from A to Z including videos, commercials and short films, from sound design to the soundtrack, from the storyboard to the footage and post production;
  • Creation of interactive digital videos, from summary videos to interactive applications for performances or video games;
  • Research on new interactive and experimental digital/electroacoustic instruments, from purely musical creations to smartphone applications, from analysis and management of databases for interactive audio to artificial intelligence, from human/machine interaction to medical applications;< /li>
  • Wide-ranging aesthetic reflection on the composition and creativity of the temporal arts, from stimuli coming from other arts to those deriving from contemporary physics;
  • Study of acoustics with particular attention to perception;
  • Study of the history of electronic music;
  • Study of electronic music analysis;

TEACHING CREATIVITY

At GRAIM the teachers pursue a teaching of creativity which, after providing the theoretical and practical foundations, puts the student in a realistic professional situation in which he must find a perfectly functioning solution for the required creative and technical purposes by his work or by a client. Nothing needs to be "user-proof" like technological and multimedia applications, be it a professional musician, ephemeral user, spectator or buyer.
A particular and delicate work is carried out on ideas (musical, interactive, installation, video, algorithmic), from the initial inspiration, through the organic development of its consequences, up to the final realization. The tutoring of creativity involves fundamental listening to the student, the reasons for his inspiration, his path, how he carries out his creative work by tackling the issues and the problems that the creation of a work entails.

MASTERCLASS

In addition to this activity, we have regularly invited teachers, external artists and companies from the world of professional audio to hold masterclasses, including: Andrea Valle, Alessandro Petrolati, Enrico Ascoli, Attila Faravelli, Nicola Ratti, Sarah Fdili Alaoui, Marco Fusi and the companies Meyersound, MIDAS, DPA microphones, Ableton University.

RESEARCH

At GRAIM the research activity is in notable development, originating and stimulated by the professional experience of teachers in prestigious international electronic music research centers such as IRCAM in Paris , the CNMAT in Berkeley University of California, the CNRS - Collège de France, the Centre Henri Pousseur in Liège, the HEM in Geneva, etc.
Some GRAIM students have made their way by developing particular research projects; a notable example is ATTACCO, by Sergio Zacco, or the XY Coordinates Generator Pack by Daniele Fabris, published among the Packages of Max, one of the leading programming environments used by the electronic and digital music community.
GRAIM is currently developing GraimTools, a package of applications for live electronic music which will be released in the near future. Some are already available, such as Decenter, Gr4imulator and GRAIM Noise.
