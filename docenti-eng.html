FACULTY

Lorenzo Pagliei

Composer, pianist, electroacoustic musician, and conductor. Since 2005, he has been living in Paris where he worked at IRCAM, Centre Pompidou, as a composer in research (2008-2017) and as a teacher in the Cursus program for composition and musical informatics (2015-17 and 2022-23). Graduated in Piano, Composition, Conducting, and Electronic Music, he studied composition with Azio Corghi, Ivan Vandor, Salvatore Sciarrino, and Alessandro Solbiati; electronic music with Giorgio Nottoli; and conducting with Dario Lucantoni. Graduated with honors from the Advanced Specialization Course in Composition (PhD) at the Accademia Nazionale di Santa Cecilia in Rome (jury president Luciano Berio, with whom he later had a fruitful period of dialogue about his music). He attended composition masterclasses with Gérard Grisey, Helmut Lachenmann, Brian Ferneyhough, Jonathan Harvey, Tristan Murail, Yan Maresz, Philippe Manoury, Henri Pousseur, and Hugues Dufourt. During his research at IRCAM, he designed and built a family of electroacoustic instruments: the Geecos (Gesture Controlled Surfaces). These instruments allow the musician to synthesize sound in real-time by modulating its parameters through hand gestures performed on specially crafted wooden surfaces. In collaboration with CNRS – Collège de France and composer-researcher Carmine Emanuele Cella, he developed a sound synthesis and sonification system using data from sub-molecular particle experiments: Microp (Micro-particle Synthesis). This real-time functioning system has been used for a sound installation, various performances, and pedagogical activities (Centre des Arts d'Enghien-les-Bains, Paris 2011, and Ville d'Orsay 2015). He is active as a composer of instrumental, vocal, and electronic music and as a performer/improviser with electroacoustic instruments and piano. He has composed for and collaborated with prestigious performers and institutions such as Orchestre Philharmonique de Radio France, Orchestra della Toscana, Orchestra di Padova e del Veneto, Ensemble Court-circuit, Divertimento Ensemble, Mdi Ensemble, EXAUDI vocal ensemble, Quatuor Danel, Quatuor Tana, Ars Ludi percussion ensemble, Francesco Dillon, Arne Deforce, Alexis Descharmes, Jean Deroyer, Daniel Ciampolini, Séverine Ballon, Laura Faoro, Laura Catrani, Simone Beneventi, Marco Fusi, Francesco Filidei, Antonio Caggiano, Gabriele Cassone, Andrea Corazziari, LeineRoebana dance company, Ministero della Cultura francese, Ernst von Siemens Foundation, BBC Radio 3, la Biennale di Venezia, Musica Strasbourg, Ars Musica Brussels, Wien Modern, Voix Nouvelles Royaumont, Accademia di Santa Cecilia, Accademia Chigiana, IUC - Istituzione Universitaria Concerti di Roma, RomaEuropa festival, Accademia di Francia Villa Medici, Festival Aperto Reggio Emilia, Nuova Consonanza, Teatro dell’Opera di Roma, Arena di Verona, Huddersfield Contemporary Music Festival, IRCAM, Centre Pompidou, CNRS - Collège de France, etc. He was co-artistic director of the Centre Henri Pousseur in Liège dedicated to mixed electronic music. He curated the artistic direction of the XXI Musicale record series dedicated to electronic music, distributed by EMI Music Italy and later by Halidon, in collaboration with Radio Rai and IMAIE. His music has been published from 2001 to 2013 by Edizioni Suvini-Zerboni and since 2013 by Casa Ricordi – Milano. In 2003, he founded the Electronic Music Course at the Conservatory of Vicenza (GRAIM) where he teaches Electroacoustic Musical Composition and Design of Virtual Instruments for Live Electronics. He is regularly invited to give masterclasses at various institutions: IRCAM Centre Pompidou Paris, Conservatoire National Supérieur de Musique et Danse de Lyon, le Fresnoy Studio National des Arts Contemporains, ESMUC of Barcelona, Università “Tor Vergata” – Rome, and various Italian conservatories. His music is based on a conception of curved, elastic, and micro-chaotic time, on nuanced synchronization, and the layering of different types of time in fluid relation (poly-temporality). He is developing flexible writing techniques in which the creative contribution of the performer is essential. The aim is to achieve the freshness and vitality of the musical present, typical of improvisation, while articulating a precise large-scale architecture. In electronic music, he seeks to create what is impossible to achieve with acoustic instruments: the synthesis of unrealistic or physically inspired sounds (Phygital), gestural control of sound, hyper-amplification of objects or acoustic instruments, the use of immersive spatialization to add new dramaturgical-psychological dimensions to listening. Another focus is the voice, conceived not only as singing but also as a source of multiple behaviors. For this reason, his vocal scores are situated at the fertile intersection of music, theater, and literature.

Luca Richelli

Sound director and composer. Graduated in Electronic Music, Composition, New Technologies, and Piano. Since 2020, he has been teaching Electroacoustic Musical Composition at the Conservatory “A. Pedrollo” in Vicenza. Previously, he taught at the conservatories of Brescia, Como, Ferrara, Padua, Trento, and Venice. He has taught Sound Design courses at the Department of New Technologies at the Academy of Fine Arts in Brera (Milan) and Computer Music at the Hochschule für Musik und Darstellende Kunst in Stuttgart. In the field of live electronics, he has performed both his compositions and contemporary repertoire at numerous festivals including: La Biennale di Venezia 2024, 2021, 2013, and 2012, Nodo Festival Ostrava 2022 (Czech Republic), Festival Milano Musica 2020 and 2017, Ars Musica Brussels (Belgium) 2018, Festival Traiettorie Parma 2017 and 2014, Colloqui di Informatica Musicale Ancona 2022, Udine 2018, Cagliari 2016, Rome 2014, Turin 2010 and Venice 2008, 18th Slowind Festival 2016 Ljubljana (Slovenia), Rassegne SaMPL and Centro d’Arte degli studenti dell’Università di Padova 2013-2019, Auditorium Parco della Musica Rome 2013, Festival Internazionale di Musica Contemporanea, Sound and Music Computing Padua 2011, EMUfest Rome 2009-2014, Verona Contemporanea 2010, Mondi Sonori Trento 2009, Festival Spaziomusica Cagliari 2008, ArteScienza Biennale Internazionale di arte scienza e cultura contemporanea Rome 2008, Variazioni di Pressione Verona 2008 and 2005, Stagioni ExNovo Musica Venice 2014, 2013, and 2007. On commission from IRCAM in Paris, he wrote the online manual for the OMChroma library for the OpenMusic composition assistance graphical environment.

Davide Tiso

Sound designer, composer, and pianist. He has created musical works, film soundtracks, interactive performances, and multimedia installations, collaborating with Giorgio Battistelli, Adriano Guarnieri, Claudio Ambrosini, Giacomo Manzoni, Fabio Vacchi, Stefano Taglietti, Paolo Furlani, Carlo De Pirro, Mauro Montalbetti, and Pietro Pirelli. He has performed at the Opera National du Rhin in Strasbourg, Teatro Odéon du Paris, De Vlaamse Opera in Antwerp, Almeida in London, Teatro La Fenice in Venice, Biennale di Venezia, Orchestra Sinfonica della RAI in Turin, Ravenna Festival, Maggio Musicale Fiorentino, ORT Orchestra regionale della Toscana, Fondazione Musica per Roma, and the Brisbane Festival Australia. He has been a member of the board of Agon Ars Magnetica in Milan; he is a part of the Arazzi Laptop Ensemble; he teaches “Sound Design” at the Accademia di Belle Arti in Venice and at the Conservatory of Ferrara, and he conducts the “Laboratorio di Produzione ed Installazione Multimediale” at the University of Bologna; he has been part of the scientific committee of AIMI, the Italian Association of Computer Music. Founder of Audiofficine and happytobehappywith™.

Paolo Furlani

He is the author of 12 operas, 9 of which have been performed: El roverso mondo, from Ruzante (Udine 1995), Il teatrino delle maraviglie, from Cervantes (Lugo 1995), Le parole al buio, from P. Puppa (Spoleto 1998), Incanto di Natale, libretto by R. Diana, from Dickens (Rovigo and Mestre 2000 and 2004), Singin’ in the Brain, libretto by E. Barbalich, from O. Sachs (Ferentino 2000), La casa dei mostri, libretto by M. Vago (Venice 2003 and 2006, Genoa 2005, Florence 2007 and 2014), Il principe granchio, libretto by M. Chiarenza (Modena 2006), Il vestito nuovo dell’imperatore, libretto by G. Rodari (Savona 2009, Genoa 2010, Incheon, South Korea, 2010, Venice and Pordenone 2012-2013), The Water Babies – Bambini acquatici, libretto by P. Lynch, from Kingsley (Rovigo 2014).
These operas have been produced by theaters such as La Fenice in Venice, Carlo Felice in Genoa, Maggio Musicale Fiorentino, Comunale “L. Pavarotti” in Modena, and Teatro Sociale in Rovigo.
He has won important national and international composition awards with his works: “Wien Modern 2000”; “Orpheus 1998” from the Teatro Lirico Sperimentale in Spoleto; Anima Mundi in Pisa (2022).
His symphonic pieces have been performed at the Biennale di Venezia (2004), by the Orchestra Sinfonica di Milano (2019), and by the Orchestra di Padova e del Veneto (2023). He has collaborated with Teatro Stabile Veneto, creating some stage music and a melologue, with the Orchestra of Teatro La Fenice in Venice.

Laura Zattra

Laura Zattra deals with sound and music of the 20th and 21st centuries, with an interest in the history and connections between art, science, and technology, electroacoustic music, collaborative practices, and women composers. Using a methodology that combines the study of sources, analysis, oral history, ethnography, and sociology, she has conducted research on works (J. Chowning, L. Nono, A. Di Scipio, S. Sciarrino, J.C. Risset, J. Harvey, Y. Höller, M. Graziani, W. Motz, F. Razzi, sound artists...), authors (T. Rampazzi, R. Zatti), centers (CSC of Padua, IRCAM of Paris, CCRMA of Stanford), collaborations (Fabbriciani-Nono, musical assistants, sound designers, the birth of Spectralism), instruments (Nagra, MUSIC programs...), and emerging professional figures (musical assistants, sound designers' practices). After earning a diploma in flute and a degree in Literature and Philosophy, she obtained two doctorates at the University of Paris Sorbonne (M. Battier) and the University of Trento (R. Dalmonte). She was a Post-Doc and later a Senior Researcher at the University of Padua (2004-2012), an invited researcher at CNRS (Centre national de la recherche scientifique) in 2012 at IRCAM in Paris, and since then, an associated researcher at the APM Analyse des Pratiques Musicales and PDS Perception et design sonores teams (IRCAM, Paris). She currently collaborates with IRCAM on the RAMHO project (Recherche et Acoustique Musicales en France: une Histoire Orale). Over the years, she has also collaborated with, among others, De Monfort University, Calgary University, Archivio Luigi Nono, the Master Sound Studies and Sonic Arts at the Universität der Künste in Berlin, the Computational Sonology Center of the University of Padua, and the NoMus Association, in research projects or for teaching. She regularly participates in international conferences as a speaker, moderator, and keynote speaker. She teaches various academic courses at the I and II levels and in master's programs. She is the founder and administrator of the website www.teresarampazzi.it, a member of the editorial board of the journal Musica/Tecnologia (Firenze University Press) and the Analyses project (IRCAM, Paris). She is also a reviewer for scientific journals and conferences in Italy and abroad (EMS, SMC, CIM, Mundo Sonoro, NOVA, ...), and an evaluator of projects (Evaluator of Project Proposals for the European Community and MIUR). She is the author of, among others, Designing Tools and Devices (with Alessandretti & Zavagna, 2021), La Notazione della Musica Elettroacustica (with Alessandretti, 2019), Live-Electronic Music. Composition, Performance and Study (with Sallis, Bertolani, Burle, 2018); Studiare la Computer Music (2011); Presenza Storica di Luigi Nono (with De Benedictis, 2011); Vent’anni di Musica Elettronica all’Università di Padova. Il Centro di Sonologia Computazionale (with Durante, 2002).

Andrea Carli

Professional sound engineer since 1996, with extensive experience in various musical genres and a specialization in the symphonic environment, both live and in the recording studio. Multiple musical genres, from rock to jazz, from electronic to acoustic music. However, his true vocation has been revealed in the field of symphonic music, both live and in studio recordings, collaborating with the most prestigious orchestras and conductors worldwide.
