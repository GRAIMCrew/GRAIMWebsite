ENTRY REQUIREMENTS:

To be admitted to a second level academic diploma course it is necessary to possess one of the qualifications listed below upon enrolment:

  • Conservatory diploma under the previous system (together with the upper secondary school diploma or another qualification obtained abroad and recognized as suitable);
  • First level academic diploma;
  • First level academic qualification obtained abroad;
  • Degree from the previous system;
  • Bachelor's degree;
  • Degree obtained abroad.
Enrollment with reservation is permitted if the required qualification is obtained by the last session of the academic year preceding the year of enrolment.

EDUCATIONAL GOALS:

Upon obtaining the title, Masters Graduates in Electronic Music have developed:

  • Artistic personality and high professional level that allows them to realize and express their own artistic concepts;
  • A full mastery of the styles and techniques developed with new technologies in the audiovisual and multimedia fields, such as to allow them to enter in a vast range of different contexts, including multidisciplinary ones;
  • Technological, IT, artistic and professional skills adequate to international standards of excellence in the musical field and in artistic production in general, aimed at interacting in international systems of the working environment;
  • Full mastery of both technical and aesthetic musical analysis;
  • Ability to compose music in various fields, styles and with a distinctive and personal character, as well as to further and autonomously develop one's aesthetic tendencies in a particular area of specialization;
  • To relate knowledge with practical skills to strengthen one's artistic development;
  • To operate creatively using new technologies;
  • To represent one's artistic concepts in writing and verbally;
  • To undertake artistic projects in new or unknown contexts in an organized way, also in staff with experts from other artistic areas.

CARREER OUTCOMES:

The course offers the student employment opportunities in the following areas:

  • Electroacoustic music composition;
  • Musical professions connected with sound technologies;
  • Musical professions connected with multimedia events.

COURSE PROGRAM

First year

Electroacoustics

Provide students with the skills needed to design and manage audio production systems. Audio System Design. Sound Diffusion System Design. Integration of Electroacoustic Systems in Real Environments. Mixing Techniques for Stereophonic and Ambisonic Systems.

Informatic Musical Composition I

Tutoring of complex compositional projects. Development of creativity. Selection and development of a musical research project in agreement with the teacher.

Polymodal and Interactive Executive Environments I

To be defined.

Systems and Programming Languages ​​for Audio and Musical Applications I

The aim of the course is to provide theoretical knowledge and practical tools in the field of Computer-Aided Composition (CAC). The programming environment used is the BACH library of the Max 8 graphical programming environment.

Tools and Methods for Bibliographical Research

The course aims to provide the knowledge necessary to conduct bibliographic research in the field of music and musicology, promoting the conscious and targeted use of paper and computer tools, with particular reference to those present in the Institute Library, acquiring the ability to evaluate, organize and communicate the results obtained.

Techniques for Musical Improvisation I

The course aims to design and develop an electronic improvisation performance environment, aimed at live performance.

Philosophy of Music

To be defined.

Gestures and Stage Movement

The course aims to explore movement and its qualities, in the context of electroacoustic performance.

EU Foreign Language (English)

Develop basic language skills in Reading, Writing, Speaking. Consolidate grammatical structures and vocabulary (Grammar and Vocabulary activities).

Second year

Acoustics

Provide students with the skills needed to design and manage immersive audio diffusion systems. Design and Management of Audio Systems for Immersive Audio.

History of Electroacoustic Music

The course aims to delve into various key moments in the history of electroacoustic music, from Sound Art to the use of Sound Design in cinema.

Polymodal and Interactive Executive Environments II

To be defined.

Informatic Musical Composition II

Tutoring of complex compositional projects. Development of creativity. Selection and development of a musical research project in agreement with the teacher.

Systems and Programming Languages ​​for Audio and Musical Applications II

Design and implementation of a system - hardware/software - for interacting with the real world via sensors and actuators. The hardware/software programming environment used is the Arduino micro-controller.

Live Performance Law and Legislation

The course aims to provide students with knowledge of the fundamental elements of public and private law, in particular copyright, related rights, the different types of employment relationships with particular attention to the main contractual forms in the entertainment world. During the course, we will also proceed with a critical analysis of the problems and solutions to be adopted in reference to the stipulation of contracts.

Techniques for Musical Improvisation II

The course aims to design and develop an electronic improvisation performance environment, aimed at live performance.
