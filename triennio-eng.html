ENTRY REQUIREMENTS:

High school diploma or another qualification obtained abroad and recognized as suitable (required upon enrolment).
In the case of particularly strong artistic qualities, the candidate may be admitted even if he does not have a high school diploma which, however, must be obtained before taking the final test.
Admission is subject to passing the selection test which verifies knowledge in the field of electronic and electroacoustic music and basic scientific and technical knowledge. After enrolment, the student will take a test of knowledge and skills of basic musical education in the following areas:

  • Use of musical notation, knowledge of the fundamental elements of music theory and exercise of the fundamental skills relating to listening and rhythmic and sung reading.

EDUCATIONAL GOALS:

At the end of the studies relating to the bachelor's degree in Electronic Music, students must have acquired specific technical and cultural skills such as to allow them to concretely realize their own artistic idea.
To this end, particular emphasis will be given to the study of the historical and contemporary electroacoustic musical repertoire and to the in-depth analysis of its own analytical methodologies, including the history of electroacoustic technologies and computer science applied to music. These objectives must also be achieved by encouraging the development of perceptive hearing and memorization skills, in particular in the context of the sound materials typical of the electroacoustic musical repertoire.
At the end of the three-year period, students must have acquired in-depth knowledge of the general stylistic, historical and aesthetic aspects relating to the specific field. Furthermore, with reference to the specificity of the course, the student must possess adequate skills concerning the use of electroacoustic and IT instruments as well as design skills relating to installations dedicated to the performance of the specific repertoire. The training objective of the course is also the acquisition of adequate skills relating to a second community language.

CARREER OUTCOMES:

The course offers the student employment opportunities in the following areas:

  • Electroacoustic music composition;
  • Musical professions connected with sound technologies;
  • Musical professions connected with multimedia events.

COURSE PROGRAM

First year

History and Historiography of Music (20th Century)

Knowledge of the main trends and expressions in 20th century music.

Musical Acoustics I

Theoretical principles, units of measurement, quantities, parameters, production, propagation and perception mechanisms of sound. Auditory phenomena, the acoustics of instruments and environments and tuning systems.

Electroacoustics I

Analogue and digital signals, possibility of creative and improving intervention on the signal. Knowledge and use of electroacoustic devices designed to manipulate the dynamics, timbre, and spatialization of sound. Processing phases of a musical project (audio recording, editing, mixing and pre-mastering).

Ear Training I

Enhancement of auditory skills, through educational proposals focused on analytical listening. The training course has as its primary objective the education of the musical ear, with the aim of exercising and training to recognize and discriminate certain components of sound data through auditory analysis.

Technologies and Techniques of Audio and Audio for Video Editing and Post Production I

The concepts of editing and post production in cinema and television. Audio-video synchronization: techniques, software and hardware. Post production chain process: DAW, plug-ins, filters, dynamics processors, effects.

Composition I (Computer Music)

Bring the student closer to the compositional activity starting from individual experiences to arrive at the production of a form, independently of the language used.

Programming Environments for Live Electronics I

The course aims to enable the student to acquire the ability to design performance systems for live electronic music, to create their own songs, those of others or live performances/improvisations.

Sampling, Synthesis and DSP I

The course aims to provide the student with theoretical and practical knowledge of all the techniques of synthesis, sampling, treatment, analysis and spatialization of sound.

Electroacoustic Musical Composition I

The course aims to enable the student to acquire mastery of the composition process and to guide him in all the steps of creating electroacoustic songs of different types and complexity.

Analysis of Electroacoustic Music I

Achievement of adequate competence in the management and definition of certain analytical-discursive experiences to develop and deepen the ability to deal with repertoire areas of specific interest, with the use of appropriate methodological tools, in order to encourage an increasingly analytical and interpretative mindset.

Audiovisual Composition I

The course aims to enable the student to acquire the ability to analyze and design an audiovisual product in total independence. The student will apply all the techniques learned ranging from music-image synchronization to intermediate recuts up to the planning, shooting and editing of his own work.

Community Foreign Language (English)

Develop basic language skills of Reading, Writing, Speaking. Consolidate grammatical structures and vocabulary (Grammar and Vocabulary activities).
